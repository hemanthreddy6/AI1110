\textbf{Question: } In a game, a man wins a rupee for a six and loses a rupee for any other number when a fair die is thrown. The man decided to throw a die thrice but to quit as and when he gets a six. Find the expected value of the amount he wins / loses.

\textbf{Solution: }
Let $X$ and $Y$ be two random variables which describes the event of getting six in a die roll and number of die rolls at which he quits
\begin{table}[h!]
	\input{tables/table1.tex}
	\caption{}
	\label{tab:table1}
\end{table}

$Y\in{\{1,2,3\}}$

\begin{align}
	p_{X}(1)&=\frac{1}{6}\\
	p_{X}(0)&=\frac{5}{6}
\end{align}

Let $P(n)$ denote the amount he wins if he throws the die $n$ times.

\begin{align}
	P(n) = \begin{cases}
			2-n ,  & \text{if he get a six in } n^{th} \text{ die roll} \\
			-3  ,  & \text{if } n=3 \text{ and he doesn't get six anytime} \\
			\end{cases}
\end{align}

Let $E(P(n))$ be the expected value of amount he wins in this game

\begin{align*}
	E(P(n))&=\sum_{n=1}^{n=3} {p_Y(n) \times P(n)} \\
			&=p_X(1) \times (2-1) + p_X(0) \times p_X(1) \times (2-2) +\\ &      p_X(0)^2 \times p_X(1) \times (2-3) + p_X(0)^3 \times -3 \\
			&=\frac{1}{6} + \frac{0}{36} - \frac{25}{216} - \frac{375}{216}\\
			&=-\frac{364}{216}\\
			&\approx -1.685\\
			&\therefore~ \text{The expected amount he wins is} \approx -1.685
\end{align*}
